# Lisbon - Alfama Vintage Market Walking Tour With Tastings

## 1. Summary

Small-group food and culture walk through Graça and Alfama. In around three hours you combine viewpoints, hidden streets and traditional taverns with tastings of classic Lisbon food and drink. Designed for curious travellers who want stories, not just snacks.

### 1.1 Key facts

- Duration: about 3 hours on foot
- Group size: minimum 4, maximum 8 guests
- Operating days: Tuesday and Saturday (aligned with Alfama's vintage market days)
- Language: EN / NL  (one language per group)
- Pace: relaxed, with regular sit-down breaks
- Walking distance: roughly 2.5–3 km, on cobblestones and some hills

## 2. Why book this tour

- Experience Alfama like a local, not a checklist.
- Mix of viewpoints, markets, taverns and everyday streets.
- Three to four curated food and drink stops instead of random snacks.
- Enough history to understand Lisbon without feeling like a lecture.
- Small groups so everyone can see, hear and ask questions.

## 3. Highlights (for cards and SEO)

- Miradouro da Graça – wide views over Castelo, river and Baixa.
- Slow descent through Graça and Alfama backstreets.
- Local tavern stop at Tabernita for petiscos and drinks.
- Traditional beer hall atmosphere at Cervejaria Amigo António.
- Pastel de nata stop at a Manteigaria bakery.
- Mercado / vintage stalls around Mercado de Santa Clara on market days.
- Azulejo details, miradouros and everyday Alfama life along the way.

## 4. Sample itinerary

> This is a working outline for training and content. Exact timings and order can be adjusted once the route is fully tested on the ground.

- **00:00 – Meet & greet**  
  Welcome, safety, quick expectations check, first intro to Alfama history.

- **00:20 – Miradouro da Graça**  
  First viewpoint, explain city layout, 1755 earthquake, neighbourhoods.

- **00:40 – Descent through Graça backstreets**  
  Short stories about everyday Lisbon life, street art, calçada portuguesa.

- **01:00 – Food & drink stop 1 – Tabernita**  
  Petiscos-style tasting and drink, introduction to Portuguese bar culture.

- **01:40 – Alfama lanes towards Feira da Ladra / Mercado de Santa Clara**  
  Vintage market or surrounding streets, depending on day and time.

- **02:00 – Food & drink stop 2 – Cervejaria Amigo António**  
  Local cervejaria feel, small dishes and drinks; short stories about taverns.

- **02:30 – Walk towards Baixa / Manteigaria**  
  Transition from old Alfama into downtown area.

- **02:45 – Food & drink stop 3 – Manteigaria**  
  Pastel de nata and coffee-style drink, explanation of sweets and bakeries.

- **03:00 – Closing**  
  Recap, questions, suggestions for where to go next.

## 5. Food and drink concept

### 5.1 Included tastings (baseline idea)

- Savoury petiscos selection at Tabernita (to be finalised with venue).
- One drink per guest at Tabernita (wine, beer or soft drink).
- Shared plates and drinks at Cervejaria Amigo António.
- One pastel de nata per guest at Manteigaria + coffee or similar drink.

### 5.2 Possible upgrades / options

- Add premium wine or ginjinha tasting near Rossio / Baixa.
- Swap or add venues on busy days or for private groups.
- Vegetarian / pescetarian variations where possible.

## 8. Know before you go

- **Fitness level:** suitable for guests with average fitness who can walk for 3 hours with breaks.
- **Terrain:** cobblestones, some slopes and stairs in Graça and Alfama.
- **Weather:** tour runs in light rain; heavy rain plan and rescheduling to be defined.
- **Shoes:** closed, comfortable walking shoes strongly recommended.
- **Dietary needs:** allergies and dietary restrictions must be shared at booking; not all venues can adapt.
- **Accessibility:** hilly, uneven ground – not suitable for all mobility needs.
